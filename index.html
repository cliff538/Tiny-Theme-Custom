People Pledge, Kindness, and Professionalism

Jan 27, 2025

I have a nice little button in the footer of my website called The People Pledge. I believe in those words and am constantly striving to fulfill them. However, I’ve been thinking about removing that pledge and improving it, making it my own, which would include some essential words like kindness, respect, and love.

In all the big online piss-up last week, some of the things I saw missing were kindness and respect. That pledge says, “I commit to treating others fairly.” I’m not sure I saw that either. But I’m just one person. I don’t know all the history and nuance of this case, but it was deeply disturbing to me; truthfully, I almost deleted my account, my mastodon, my website, and my online life and ran from what I thought was a good place, and I wasn’t even the focus! I can’t imagine the pain, hurt, and sleepless nights had by most, if not all, involved. It doesn’t have to be this way.

In my work life, we have a thing called Professional Standards. It’s a way to reduce employee friction and leave the company out of the process. It lets employees with some training call each person and get their side of the story, then bring the two people together for a sit-down, phone call, or Zoom to work things out. It’s my understanding that the success rate with this is exceptionally high. Things are done with kindness and respect; they are settled among the employees themselves and leave the company and the public out of the process that usually has legal restrictions that may include punishment, etc., that don’t need to be if the employees themselves handle it. It’s a win-win for everybody. Perhaps even in cases like last week, there could be something of the sort?

People make mistakes. They learn and grow from being called out kindly and gently. I, for one, have come a long, long way in becoming a better human to all. My wife calls me out frequently, and it’s painful, but it makes me a better human being. I work in a very diverse company and have respect, love, and kindness for all, but it didn’t start out that way, I had some rough edges and needed to learn about things that I knew nothing about. But after 56 years of living, I’ve learned that it provokes hostility if it’s done with hostility. Does having hundreds of people gang up on you help? Some things, most sensitive things, are better done out of the public view, especially when it’s done within your tribe. It’s worthwhile to start there and at least try, is it not?

I don’t mean any disrespect to anyone, and I fully admit there is history here that I’m not privy to. There are always more sides, hurt feelings, and people involved than I even know, but you can still include kindness and professionalism, especially when you have a People Pledge to follow.

I also understand that some become frustrated by people’s actions, especially when directed at an already attacked group, and just don’t have the bandwidth for civility or kindness. I’ve been there. We are humans and far from perfect. That’s why this is just a reminder about civility and kindness. In a world chock full of hate, the thing that we don’t need less of is kindness.

The attacks are real, and the hostility is growing by the day with this new administration. Let’s work together to do what each of us can. Let’s be kind to one another and protect everyone that we can through actions and words.
